Psychotherapy Services

Evidence-based therapeutic approaches for lasting positive change

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT is a structured, goal-oriented therapy that helps individuals identify and change negative thought patterns and behaviors. It's highly effective for treating anxiety, depression, and various mental health conditions.

Key Components:

  • • Cognitive restructuring and thought challenging
  • • Behavioral activation and exposure therapy
  • • Skills training and homework assignments
  • • Problem-solving and coping strategies

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

DBT combines cognitive-behavioral techniques with mindfulness practices. It's particularly effective for individuals with emotional dysregulation, borderline personality disorder, and self-harm behaviors.

Four Modules:

  • • Mindfulness skills
  • • Distress tolerance techniques
  • • Emotional regulation strategies
  • • Interpersonal effectiveness skills

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

ACT helps individuals accept difficult thoughts and feelings while committing to actions that align with their values. It's effective for anxiety, depression, and chronic pain.

Core Processes:

  • • Psychological flexibility training
  • • Values clarification and goal setting
  • • Mindfulness and acceptance practices
  • • Committed action planning

Family Therapy

Family therapy addresses relationship dynamics and communication patterns within families. It's effective for resolving conflicts, improving communication, and supporting family members through difficult times.

Approaches:

  • • Structural family therapy
  • • Strategic family therapy
  • • Narrative family therapy
  • • Parent management training
